## SECTION 1: Governance & Compliance (Questions 1-25)

**Q1.** What three characteristics define a community association in Florida?  
A. Voluntary membership, right to vote, annual meetings  
B. Mandatory membership, right to vote, assessments can become a lien  
C. Mandatory membership, board elections, reserve funding  
D. Property ownership, annual meetings, insurance requirements  
**Answer:** B — The three pillars are mandatory membership, right to vote, and assessments that can become a lien if unpaid.

---

**Q2.** Which Florida Statute is known as the "Not-for-Profit Corporation Act"?  
A. FS 468  
B. FS 617  
C. FS 718  
D. FS 720  
**Answer:** B — FS 617 governs how associations incorporate as not-for-profit corporations.

---

**Q3.** A condominium is a form of ownership that includes:  
A. Shares in a corporation that owns the building  
B. Exclusive ownership of a unit plus an undivided share of common elements  
C. A right to use property for a specific time period  
D. Ownership of a lot within a planned community  
**Answer:** B — Condo ownership = your unit + shared ownership of common elements (lobby, pool, etc.).

---

**Q4.** The Declaration of Condominium is BEST described as:  
A. The operating procedures for the association  
B. The document that creates the condominium and defines units and common elements  
C. The rules adopted by the board of directors  
D. The financial plan for the association  
**Answer:** B — The Declaration is the "constitution" — it creates the condo and is recorded with the county.

---

**Q5.** In the hierarchy of governing documents, which has the HIGHEST authority?  
A. Declaration of Condominium  
B. Bylaws  
C. Rules and Regulations  
D. Federal and State Law  
**Answer:** D — Law always trumps all governing documents. Hierarchy: Law > Declaration > Articles > Bylaws > Rules.

---

**Q6.** A developer must turn over control of a condominium association no later than:  
A. 3 years after recording the declaration  
B. 5 years after recording the declaration  
C. 7 years after recording the declaration  
D. 10 years after recording the declaration  
**Answer:** C — 7 years is the maximum. Turnover can also be triggered earlier (e.g., 3 months after 90% of units conveyed).

---

**Q7.** Which statement about the CAM license is TRUE?  
A. A CAM license is required for all associations regardless of budget  
B. A CAM license is required when the budget exceeds $100,000  
C. A CAM license is required when the budget exceeds $150,000  
D. A CAM license is only required for condominium associations  
**Answer:** B — The $100,000 threshold applies to all types of community associations.

---

**Q8.** A CAM license must be renewed every:  
A. 1 year  
B. 2 years  
C. 3 years  
D. 4 years  
**Answer:** B — Every 2 years, with 20 hours of continuing education required.

---

**Q9.** Which of the following is NOT a fiduciary duty of a board member?  
A. Duty of care  
B. Duty of loyalty  
C. Duty of profitability  
D. Duty of good faith  
**Answer:** C — Board members owe care, loyalty, and good faith. There is no "duty of profitability."

---

**Q10.** A unit owner requests to inspect official records. The association must provide access within:  
A. 5 business days  
B. 10 business days  
C. 15 business days  
D. 30 calendar days  
**Answer:** B — 10 business days of a written request for condominiums.

---

**Q11.** The maximum the association can charge for copying official records is:  
A. $0.10 per page  
B. $0.15 per page  
C. $0.25 per page  
D. $0.50 per page  
**Answer:** C — $0.25 per page is the statutory maximum.

---

**Q12.** Which of the following records may an association withhold from member inspection?  
A. Board meeting minutes  
B. Attorney-client privileged communications  
C. Annual budget  
D. Insurance policies  
**Answer:** B — Attorney-client privilege is one of the few exceptions to the open records requirement.

---

**Q13.** A prospectus is:  
A. The annual financial report  
B. A disclosure document given to buyers before purchase  
C. The management contract  
D. The reserve study  
**Answer:** B — The prospectus discloses key information to prospective buyers.

---

**Q14.** Board members of a condominium association are required to:  
A. Hold a CAM license  
B. Be residents of the community  
C. Certify they have read the governing documents or complete board education  
D. Have a college degree  
**Answer:** C — Board members must certify they've read the documents or complete the required education course.

---

**Q15.** The DBPR Division of Florida Condominiums, Timeshares, and Mobile Homes regulates:  
A. HOAs under FS 720  
B. Condominiums under FS 718  
C. Real estate brokers  
D. Building contractors  
**Answer:** B — DBPR regulates condos, co-ops, and timeshares. It does NOT regulate HOAs.

---

**Q16.** Which entity has the authority to adopt rules and regulations for the community?  
A. The CAM  
B. The board of directors  
C. Individual unit owners  
D. The management company  
**Answer:** B — The board adopts rules. The CAM implements them but does not create them.

---

**Q17.** A CAM may do all of the following EXCEPT:  
A. Prepare meeting agendas  
B. Provide legal advice to the board  
C. Coordinate maintenance activities  
D. Prepare financial reports  
**Answer:** B — A CAM is NOT an attorney and cannot give legal advice.

---

**Q18.** Financial records of the association must be retained for a minimum of:  
A. 3 years  
B. 5 years  
C. 7 years  
D. 10 years  
**Answer:** C — Financial records must be kept for at least 7 years.

---

**Q19.** FS 720 governs which type of community association?  
A. Condominiums  
B. Cooperatives  
C. Homeowners Associations  
D. Timeshares  
**Answer:** C — FS 720 = Homeowners' Association Act.

---

**Q20.** In a cooperative (FS 719), the owner holds:  
A. A deed to their individual unit  
B. Shares in a corporation that owns the building  
C. A timeshare interest  
D. A leasehold interest only  
**Answer:** B — Co-op owners own shares in the corporation, not the unit itself.

---

**Q21.** The Articles of Incorporation are filed with:  
A. The county recorder's office  
B. The Florida Division of Corporations  
C. The DBPR  
D. The local building department  
**Answer:** B — Articles are filed with the FL Division of Corporations under FS 617.

---

**Q22.** Purchasers of a timeshare plan have a rescission (cancellation) period of:  
A. 3 days  
B. 7 days  
C. 10 days  
D. 15 days  
**Answer:** C — 10-day cooling-off period for timeshare purchases.

---

**Q23.** Which of the following is TRUE about developer control of a condo association?  
A. The developer has no fiduciary duty during the control period  
B. The developer has the same fiduciary duties as any board member  
C. The developer can waive all reserve requirements permanently  
D. The developer is not required to maintain insurance  
**Answer:** B — The developer owes the same fiduciary duties (care, loyalty, good faith) as any board member.

---

**Q24.** Official records of the association must be maintained within:  
A. The community itself  
B. 45 miles of the community or within the county  
C. The management company's main office  
D. The state capital  
**Answer:** B — Within 45 miles of the community or within the county where the association is located.

---

**Q25.** If an association fails to provide records within the required timeframe, the consequence is:  
A. A fine from the DBPR  
B. The association must pay the requesting member's attorney fees  
C. The board members are personally liable  
D. The CAM license is suspended  
**Answer:** B — The association is liable for the member's reasonable attorney fees.

## SECTION 2: Financial Management (Questions 26-47)

**Q26.** The annual budget of a condominium association must include:  
A. Only operating expenses  
B. Only reserve funds  
C. Both operating expenses and reserves  
D. Operating expenses, reserves, and individual unit expenses  
**Answer:** C — The budget must include both operating and reserve components.

---

**Q27.** Members must receive the proposed budget at least how many days before the budget meeting?  
A. 7 days  
B. 10 days  
C. 14 days  
D. 30 days  
**Answer:** C — 14 days advance notice for budget meetings (condos and co-ops).

---

**Q28.** An assessment that covers unexpected or unusually large expenses is called a:  
A. Regular assessment  
B. Special assessment  
C. Maintenance assessment  
D. Capital assessment  
**Answer:** B — Special assessments cover unexpected or large expenses beyond the regular budget.

---

**Q29.** Before recording a lien for unpaid assessments on a condominium unit, the association must send an intent-to-lien letter at least:  
A. 10 days before  
B. 14 days before  
C. 30 days before  
D. 45 days before  
**Answer:** C — 30 days by certified mail for condominiums (FS 718).

---

**Q30.** Before recording a lien for unpaid assessments on an HOA property, the association must send notice at least:  
A. 10 days before  
B. 14 days before  
C. 30 days before  
D. 45 days before  
**Answer:** D — 45 days for HOAs (FS 720). Remember: "HOAs take longer."

---

**Q31.** A condominium association lien for unpaid assessments is effective for:  
A. 6 months  
B. 1 year  
C. 2 years  
D. 5 years  
**Answer:** B — 1 year from the date the claim of lien was recorded.

---

**Q32.** When a first mortgage holder forecloses on a condo unit, the "safe harbor" amount they owe the association is the LESSER of:  
A. 6 months of assessments or 1% of the mortgage  
B. 12 months of assessments or 1% of the original mortgage  
C. 24 months of assessments or 2% of the mortgage  
D. All past-due assessments  
**Answer:** B — Lesser of 12 months of assessments OR 1% of the original mortgage amount.

---

**Q33.** Reserve funds are set aside for items with a deferred maintenance expense of at least:  
A. $1,000  
B. $5,000  
C. $10,000  
D. $25,000  
**Answer:** C — $10,000 minimum deferred maintenance expense AND useful life of 2+ years.

---

**Q34.** Under the post-Surfside reforms, reserves for items identified in a SIRS:  
A. Can be waived by a majority vote of members  
B. Can be waived by a unanimous vote of members  
C. Can be reduced by the board  
D. Cannot be waived or reduced  
**Answer:** D — SIRS reserve items cannot be waived or reduced (effective 2025).

---

**Q35.** A condominium association with annual revenues of $200,000 is required to prepare:  
A. A report of cash receipts and expenditures  
B. Compiled financial statements  
C. Reviewed financial statements  
D. Audited financial statements  
**Answer:** B — $150,000-$299,999 = compiled financial statements.

---

**Q36.** A condominium association with annual revenues of $600,000 is required to prepare:  
A. Compiled financial statements  
B. Reviewed financial statements  
C. Audited financial statements  
D. A simple cash report  
**Answer:** C — $500,000+ = audited financial statements.

---

**Q37.** "Commingling" of funds means:  
A. Investing reserve funds in stocks  
B. Mixing association funds with personal or other non-association funds  
C. Transferring funds between operating and reserve accounts  
D. Collecting assessments in advance  
**Answer:** B — Commingling is prohibited. Association funds must be kept in separate, dedicated accounts.

---

**Q38.** Which reserve funding method uses equal annual contributions over the useful life of a component?  
A. Cash-flow method  
B. Straight-line method  
C. Threshold method  
D. Declining balance method  
**Answer:** B — Straight-line = equal annual contributions. Think "Steady Eddie."

---

**Q39.** How often should a Structural Integrity Reserve Study (SIRS) be updated?  
A. Every 3 years  
B. Every 5 years  
C. Every 10 years  
D. Every 15 years  
**Answer:** C — SIRS must be updated every 10 years.

---

**Q40.** Who is responsible for preparing the proposed annual budget?  
A. The unit owners  
B. The board president alone  
C. The manager (CAM) prepares it for board review  
D. The association's attorney  
**Answer:** C — The manager typically prepares the proposed budget, then the board reviews, adjusts, and adopts it.

---

**Q41.** Monthly financial statements should be provided to:  
A. All unit owners  
B. The board of directors  
C. The DBPR  
D. The county tax collector  
**Answer:** B — Monthly financial statements go to the board. Annual reports go to members.

---

**Q42.** Which of the following is a key internal financial control?  
A. Having one person handle all financial duties  
B. Separation of duties so no single person controls all financial functions  
C. Keeping all funds in a single account for simplicity  
D. Allowing the CAM to sign checks without board oversight  
**Answer:** B — Separation of duties is a fundamental internal control.

---

**Q43.** A fidelity bond should cover:  
A. Only the CAM  
B. Only board members  
C. All persons who control or disburse association funds  
D. Only employees  
**Answer:** C — Everyone who handles money: board members, managers, employees, management company staff.

---

**Q44.** Operating funds are used for:  
A. Roof replacement  
B. Day-to-day expenses like landscaping, utilities, and management fees  
C. Building a new clubhouse  
D. Elevator replacement  
**Answer:** B — Operating funds cover routine, recurring expenses. Major replacements come from reserves.

---

**Q45.** Checks over a certain amount should require:  
A. Only the CAM's signature  
B. Only the board president's signature  
C. Two signatures  
D. No signatures if electronic  
**Answer:** C — Two signatures for checks over the threshold amount (a key internal control).

---

**Q46.** Bank reconciliations should be performed:  
A. Annually  
B. Quarterly  
C. Monthly  
D. Only when there's a discrepancy  
**Answer:** C — Monthly bank reconciliations are a standard financial control.

---

**Q47.** Three powers granted to associations under FS 617 (Not-for-Profit Corporation Act) include:  
A. Levying taxes, issuing bonds, condemning property  
B. Incurring liabilities, borrowing funds, mortgaging association property  
C. Arresting violators, imposing jail time, seizing property  
D. Issuing stock, paying dividends, merging with other corporations  
**Answer:** B — FS 617 grants powers to incur liabilities, borrow funds, and mortgage property.

## SECTION 3: Risk Management (Questions 48-59)

**Q48.** Which type of insurance covers the structure and common elements of a condominium?  
A. HO-6  
B. Property/Hazard insurance  
C. Fidelity bond  
D. Workers' compensation  
**Answer:** B — Property/hazard insurance covers the building structure and common elements.

---

**Q49.** An HO-6 policy is carried by:  
A. The association  
B. The management company  
C. The individual unit owner  
D. The developer  
**Answer:** C — HO-6 covers the unit interior, personal property, and personal liability.

---

**Q50.** "Replacement cost" differs from "actual cash value" because:  
A. Replacement cost includes a profit margin  
B. Replacement cost does NOT deduct for depreciation  
C. Actual cash value is always higher  
D. There is no difference  
**Answer:** B — Replacement cost = full cost to replace. ACV = replacement cost minus depreciation.

---

**Q51.** Milestone inspections are required for buildings that are:  
A. 2 stories or taller  
B. 3 stories or taller  
C. 5 stories or taller  
D. 10 stories or taller  
**Answer:** B — 3 stories or taller, per FS 553.899.

---

**Q52.** A Phase 1 milestone inspection must be completed by what building age?  
A. 20 years  
B. 25 years (if within 3 miles of coast) or 30 years  
C. 35 years  
D. 40 years  
**Answer:** B — Year 25 if within 3 miles of the coastline, year 30 otherwise.

---

**Q53.** If a Phase 1 inspection reveals substantial structural deterioration, what happens next?  
A. The building is condemned  
B. A Phase 2 inspection is required  
C. The association is dissolved  
D. Nothing, it's just a recommendation  
**Answer:** B — Phase 2 provides a more detailed inspection with specific repair recommendations.

---

**Q54.** Results of a milestone inspection must be provided to unit owners within:  
A. 14 days  
B. 30 days  
C. 45 days  
D. 60 days  
**Answer:** C — 45 days after receiving the inspection results.

---

**Q55.** The SIRS must address all of the following EXCEPT:  
A. Roof  
B. Interior unit appliances  
C. Plumbing  
D. Fire protection systems  
**Answer:** B — Interior unit appliances are the owner's responsibility, not covered by SIRS.

---

**Q56.** D&O insurance protects:  
A. The building from fire damage  
B. Directors and officers from personal liability for board decisions  
C. Employees from workplace injuries  
D. The association from assessment defaults  
**Answer:** B — Directors & Officers liability insurance protects board members personally.

---

**Q57.** Which risk management strategy involves purchasing insurance?  
A. Retain  
B. Avoid  
C. Transfer  
D. Eliminate  
**Answer:** C — Transfer = shifting risk to an insurance company or another party via contract.

---

**Q58.** Workers' compensation insurance is required when the association:  
A. Has any volunteers  
B. Has employees  
C. Has a budget over $500,000  
D. Is located in a flood zone  
**Answer:** B — Workers' comp is required when the association has employees.

---

**Q59.** The association should insure common elements for:  
A. Actual cash value  
B. Original purchase price  
C. Full insurable replacement cost  
D. Tax assessed value  
**Answer:** C — Full insurable replacement cost ensures adequate coverage without depreciation deductions.

## SECTION 4: Meetings & Events (Questions 60-77)

**Q60.** Notice of a board meeting must be posted continuously for at least:  
A. 24 hours  
B. 48 hours  
C. 72 hours  
D. 7 days  
**Answer:** B — 48 hours of continuous posted notice for board meetings.

---

**Q61.** Notice of an annual members meeting must be mailed at least:  
A. 7 days in advance  
B. 10 days in advance  
C. 14 days in advance  
D. 30 days in advance  
**Answer:** C — 14 days advance mailed notice for member meetings.

---

**Q62.** The default quorum for a condominium members meeting is:  
A. 10% of voting interests  
B. 20% of voting interests  
C. 30% of voting interests  
D. 51% of voting interests  
**Answer:** C — 30% unless the bylaws specify a different percentage.

---

**Q63.** A quorum for a board meeting is typically:  
A. One-third of board members  
B. A majority of board members  
C. All board members  
D. Any two board members  
**Answer:** B — Majority of the total number of board members (e.g., 3 of 5).

---

**Q64.** Proxies may be used for board member elections in a condominium association.  
A. True  
B. False  
**Answer:** B — FALSE. Condo board elections must use secret written ballot. No proxies allowed.

---

**Q65.** A proxy for a condominium association is valid for how many days?  
A. 30 days  
B. 60 days  
C. 90 days  
D. 120 days  
**Answer:** D — 120 days from the date signed (condos). HOA proxies = 90 days.

---

**Q66.** A proxy for an HOA is valid for how many days?  
A. 30 days  
B. 60 days  
C. 90 days  
D. 120 days  
**Answer:** C — 90 days for HOAs (shorter than condos).

---

**Q67.** If the number of candidates for a condo board election equals the number of open seats:  
A. An election must still be held  
B. The candidates are seated by acclamation (no election needed)  
C. The board appoints the candidates

**Q68.** To recall a board member, the vote required is:  
A. Majority of members present at a meeting  
B. Majority of ALL voting interests

---

**Q69.** After a valid recall vote, the board must meet to certify or reject the recall within:  
A. 3 business days  
B. 5 full business days

---

**Q70.** If the board rejects a recall vote, the matter goes to:

---

**Q71.** Meeting minutes should include all of the following EXCEPT:  
A. Date and time of the meeting  
B. Motions made and vote results

D. Actions taken by the board

---

**Q72.** Draft board meeting minutes should be available within:  
A. 7 days  
B. 14 days

---

**Q74.** Proxies count toward quorum at:

---

**Q75.** The first election after developer turnover must be held within:

**Answer:** C — 90 days after turnover.

---

**Q76.** An emergency board meeting requires:  
A. 48 hours posted notice  
B. 14 days mailed notice  
C. Reasonable notice under the circumstances  
D. No notice at all  
**Answer:** C — Reasonable notice, given the emergency nature.

---

**Q77.** A board member who has a conflict of interest on a matter being voted on should:  
A. Vote anyway  
B. Abstain from voting and disclose the conflict

## SECTION 5: Property Maintenance (Questions 78-89)

**Q78.** The roof of a condominium building is classified as:  
A. A unit element  
B. A limited common element  
C. A common element  
D. An individual owner responsibility  
**Answer:** C — The roof is a common element maintained by the association.

---

**Q79.** An assigned parking space in a condo is typically classified as:  
A. A common element  
B. A limited common element  
C. Part of the unit  
D. Developer property  
**Answer:** B — Limited common element = common element reserved for use by specific unit(s).

---

**Q80.** The document that defines the boundary between association and owner maintenance responsibilities is:  
A. The bylaws  
B. The Declaration  
C. The rules and regulations  
D. The management contract  
**Answer:** B — The Declaration defines what is a unit vs. common element and who maintains what.

---

**Q81.** Preventive maintenance is important because it:  
A. Is required by federal law  
B. Extends useful life, reduces emergency repairs, and preserves property values  
C. Eliminates the need for reserves  
D. Is cheaper than insurance  
**Answer:** B — Preventive maintenance extends life, reduces emergencies, preserves values, and reduces liability.

---

**Q82.** A capital improvement differs from a maintenance repair in that it:  
A. Costs less than $1,000  
B. Adds new value or significantly extends useful life  
C. Is performed monthly  
D. Requires no board approval  
**Answer:** B — Capital improvements add value or significantly extend life; repairs restore to original condition.

---

**Q83.** When selecting a contractor for major work, best practice is to obtain:  
A. 1 bid  
B. 2 bids

**Q84.** A contractor working on association property should carry:  
A. Only a business license  
B. Liability insurance and workers' compensation

**Q85.** Material alterations to common elements in a condominium typically require approval of:

**Q86.** Work orders should be tracked to:

---

**Q87.** Fire safety systems in common areas are the responsibility of:

**Q88.** A component inventory should include:

B. All common element components with their useful life estimates  
C. Only items currently in need of repair  
D. Only items covered by insurance

**Q89.** ADA compliance in common areas is:  
A. Optional for private associations  
B. Required only for associations with 50+ units

## SECTION 6: Contracting (Questions 90-100)

**Q90.** Official records of the association belong to:  
A. The management company  
B. The CAM personally  
C. The association  
D. The board president  
**Answer:** C — Records always belong to the association, regardless of who maintains them.

---

**Q91.** A fixed-price contract means:  
A. The price can be adjusted based on actual costs  
B. The association pays a set price for a defined scope of work  
C. The contractor sets the price after completion  
D. The price is negotiated monthly  
**Answer:** B — Fixed price = set price for defined scope. Lowest risk for the association.

---

**Q92.** A "time and materials" contract:  
A. Has a fixed total price  
B. Pays the contractor for actual time spent plus materials used  
C. Is always cheaper than fixed price  
D. Requires no written agreement  
**Answer:** B — Time & materials = pay for actual time + materials. Higher risk, less predictable cost.

---

**Q93.** An indemnification clause in a contract:  
A. Sets the payment schedule  
B. Defines the scope of work  
C. Specifies that one party holds the other harmless from liability  
D. Establishes the contract term  
**Answer:** C — Indemnification = contractor holds association harmless from claims arising from their work.

---

**Q94.** Every service contract should include a:  
A. Profit-sharing agreement  
B. Termination clause  
C. Guarantee of lowest price  
D. Requirement for daily reports  
**Answer:** B — A termination clause allows either party to end the contract under specified conditions.

---

**Q95.** The CAM receives a 1099 tax form because they are typically classified as:  
A. An employee  
B. An independent contractor  
C. A board member  
D. A volunteer  
**Answer:** B — CAMs/management companies are typically independent contractors (1099), not employees (W-2).

---

**Q96.** When evaluating contractor bids, the association should consider:  
A. Only the lowest price  
B. Performance history, references, insurance, cost, and experience  
C. Only whether the contractor is local  
D. Only the contractor's availability  
**Answer:** B — Use the "PRICE" method: Performance, References, Insurance, Cost, Experience.

---

**Q97.** A management contract should specify that the manager carries:  
A. No insurance  
B. Only property insurance  
C. Errors & omissions (E&O), fidelity, and liability insurance  
D. Only workers' compensation  
**Answer:** C — E&O protects against professional mistakes, fidelity against theft, liability against injuries.
